Overview

Conducted Quality Assurance review of audit working papers and reports across FIFGROUP's nationwide branch network, assessing compliance with IPPF 2017 and GIAS 2024 standards.

Methodology

Working Paper Review: Evaluated documentation using a 3-tier scoring system (Ada-Valid / Ada-Tidak Valid / Tidak Ada) across Desk Audit and Field Audit phases for 8 scope areas: Marketing, Credit, Collateral & Fiduciary, Collection & Remedial, Inventory & Warehouse, Finance, Sales & Partnership, and Human Capital & General Service.

Audit Report Review: Assessed report quality using the 5C framework (Administrative, Clear, Complete, Concise, Consistent) on a 1-4 scale, evaluating 5W+2H completeness, narrative clarity, and evidence adequacy.

Key Findings

  • Over 40% of both desk and field audit WPs contained documentation gaps including mismatched branch headers, missing sample lists, and insufficient data analysis
  • Significant reporting gaps in Completeness and Conciseness dimensions across all reviewed months
  • Common root causes: lack of detailed SOP for audit deliverables and inconsistent ongoing monitoring

Deliverables

  • Monthly QA results reports (Aug-Nov 2025) with per-scope and per-project breakdown
  • Per-auditor performance recaps with targeted feedback
  • GIAS 2024 compliance mapping diagram
  • Management-ready consolidated QA report with findings and recommendations

HDI Regression Analysis

BPS Kota Yogyakarta • Jan - Feb 2025
13

Overview

Analyzed the influence of economic growth and education level on Human Development Index (HDI) in Kota Yogyakarta using official BPS data from 2014 to 2024.

Methodology

Variables: Dependent: Human Development Index (IPM). Independent: GDP Growth Rate (Laju Pertumbuhan PDRB) and Average Years of Schooling (Rata-rata Lama Sekolah / RLS).

Analysis: Multiple Linear Regression model to measure the individual and combined effect of each predictor on HDI variation over the 11-year period.

Key Findings

  • Education duration (RLS) had a significant positive impact on HDI
  • Economic growth (PDRB) showed no statistically significant effect on HDI
  • The model suggests that investing in education yields stronger human development outcomes than economic growth alone

Other Contributions

  • Processed and validated socioeconomic datasets from 13 districts for "Kota Yogyakarta dalam Angka 2025"
  • Designed data visualizations and publication layouts for non-technical government stakeholders
